Sometimes I try to identify the point at which I stand, the spatial and temporal co-ordinates of the position from which I'm surveying the world, to try and get a better sense of that world. Standing in a park, in a large urban sprawl, located on a damp north western archipelago of the European continent, its politics as tattered and buffeted as its coast. The sprawl itself not really of the archipelago where its located, a supra-national identity, disproportionate to the nation in which it sits, on a January day, at the end of the first quarter of the 21st century. Winter, blustery and mild, with showers gusting and blowing intermittently across the suburban streets and park spaces. 

Doing this provides an odd sense of calm. It is a place and time no more or less important than any other, but nevertheless unique. You can consider what they might be doing in Paris, Kazakhstan, or Mauritius, Accra, Tokyo or places in Peru. Or being at this particular spot in history at the tail end of so many events and tumults, which, looking out across the park you may briefly consider, whichever spring to mind, and consider our own events and tumults in turn.
