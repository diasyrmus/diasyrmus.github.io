First time to the cinema to see a good film in a long while. The experience is of course mesmeric and magical. Script,
imagery, faces, movement, the room for play of the imagination and reality, specifically the portrayal of Paris, of the
superstitious nature of Cleo and her... lady-in-waiting? (surely too grand a phrase, with maid too poor). The
superstition both a sort of fashionable accessory Cleo is trying on or discarding depending on her mood, but also a sort
of trap, which she manages to escape with the appearance of the young man in the tarot cards (a paradox, perhaps). 

The fragmenting and fracturing of her image in glass and mirrors.

Followed by a cycle ride back and meeting up with Zoe Gilbert, who wrote the really rather good *Mischief Acts*. Some
bad wine and mildly amusing conversation about Folkestone, and the Burstin Inn.
