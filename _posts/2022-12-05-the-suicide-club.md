An early dinner at Lina's meant it was still rush hour when I was due to cycle home. So I spent an hour or so in the library reading Robert Louis Stevenson's *New Arabian Nights*, which some see as the birth of the short story in English. I'm never sure how well it's known that it set the template for both Sherlock Holmes and Arthur Machen and therefore might credibly seen as one of the major influences for both the modern detective story and the modern horror story.

Its main character, Prince Florizel, seeks to overcome the tedium that a decadence of wealth has caused to fall upon him, by indulging in the equally decadent pecadillo of making an amateur study of the human race, by going abroad disguised in London to find out-of-the-ordinary adventure, by throwing himself onto chance. The need to somehow find *something*, some meaning, something to overcome an inner weariness reminded me of my point the other day –  {{2022-12-03-pere-ubu-and-other-matters.md}} – in that it has something in the nature of exploring the world around to try and set right internally what seems to have gone awry.

Sci-fi got it wrong.  
We assumed AI would be super logical and humans would provide creativity.   
But in reality it’s the opposite. Generative AI is good at getting an approximately correct output, but if you need precision and accuracy you need a human.

As we're seeing, there will be artistry in working out what extends the capabilities of AI and also what crashes it. What aesthetic thwarts it. Memetic bricolage and goblin mode moods.
