This week a dead woman smiled at me. I was cycling to work, along the
dedicated cycle lane by Rotten Row. The flow of cyclists is in the
opposite direction. I'm heading out of town, to Shepherd's Bush, the
majority of cyclists are heading into town. You see a lot of faces. On
the haul up to Kensington Gardens, I saw an old-ish woman cycling in
the opposite direction and immediately recognised her as Hilary Mason
(well, I had to look up the name later, but I recognised her),
recognised most vividly by me as one of the disconcerting, possibly
malign psychic old ladies in _Don't Look Now_. 

Like most cyclists she was looking straight ahead, but as she came up
and passed me, she gave me a most winning, delighted smile. 

The flash from 'I recognise her but from where' to 'from _there_'
happened very quickly.

I spent some seconds trying to work out whether this was likely to be
a beneficial sign - the dead from their special vantage of insight
seeing something temporarily blessed about me (temporarily from in
that very moment to for all of my living condition) - or malign, a
sort of feeling of 'you are seeing us now, and we are recognising you,
as the time of you coming to join us is not long off.'

Idle thoughts, very clearly! And easily and obviously dismissable as
someone who looked very like Hilary Mason, after all she had a cycling
helmet on, and my speculative thoughts equally lacking any sort of
sense at all.

And yet it was well past Kensington Palace and back into the congested
traffic of Kensington High Street itself before I was able to shake
off a feeling of unwelcome and undefined anticipation of something
significant coming my way.

A song on the new Bonnie Prince Billy album, _Kentucky is
Water_, Will Oldham sings the lines:

> Never try to deny a wind it's blowing
> The moment you do, your heart will be lost to eternity

These lines have woven their way through the week and brought a
reflective feeling of strength to That Which Must Change, that feeling
of augury produced by the momentary flash of an encounter.

Ah, though it's the last (truncated) week before I go on leave and
that in itself is producing feelings of change and decision. It's
unwise to hang agency of events like this. I do it all the time, and
it's not productive, or rarely persistently productive.

I'll use this weekend to _plan_ though, plan a set of activities
beyond the summer holiday, specifically Learn French, Work on a
Project, and maybe Consistently Update the Product Blog. Something
creative as well. This means ditching random reading, and focusing on
things to learn.

I can kick this off on the holiday, and ensure I have a focus and a
topic. I spent that summer in the pursuit of an idea... and I think
that idea needs to be the Grotesque, but I will also take my Cadiz
notes and maybe develop a story of sorts.
