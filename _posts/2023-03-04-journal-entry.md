Recently, things have been feeling better. Someone reached out and helped me mend myself, mentally, emotionally. To start to mend myself. 

I stopped taking SSRIs, and whether related or not, there has been more of a general sense of enjoyment. A retreat of the anhedonia I had been feeling. It is of course hard to pin anything on these feelings. I am trying not to run away with anything. Equally, I am trying not to deny agency and tangibility, a sense of possibility, with these feelings. Mirth, enjoyment at being on my bike, amusement, warmth, tenderness.

Then on Thursday, work news, fairly brutal, meant that a large amount of that fragile, nascent stability got seriously undermined. Green shoots look like they are about to undergo a winter, appropriate to the persistently cold atmospheric conditions we are experiencing after a brief sense of spring.

On Thursday evening I got drunk, and today, Saturday, I still feel tired. However, I will continue to nurture these shoots and avoid slipping back, but I can't deny some anxiety.

Astral Star Codex's [Peer Review Request: Depression](https://astralcodexten.substack.com/p/peer-review-request-depression) is a very good simple overview and helps you take a step back from your mental state.

And listening to Russ Roberts [interview](https://www.econtalk.org/paul-bloom-on-psych-psychology-and-the-human-mind/) Paul Bloom on psychology contained the useful idea of 'Thinking Like a...' discipline. Thinking Like a Psychologist in certain situations, Thinking Like an Economist. See anthropologist and novelist or artist as well.
