# how to work together 2

* Managing inclusion
* Different psychological types
* What is the information that is lost in transmission? (Benjamin v Information Theory)
* To what extend can we break down intangibles? (hormones?? food??)
* It's difficult! And i don't think there's really an answer - our own pesonal emotional contexts are less attenuated by having a shared space where to an extent we are working out of a pool of shared emotions and aims
* On the other hand, that shared space often occludes or causes psychological distress to people, who feel the need to hide who they are and how they behave.

Going out for a drink bring's people's idiorrhythms together, a convergence of spirit in a single place. Or as Kingsley Amis once said 'alcohol allows people to relax at more or less the same time'. Of course it can also exacerbate dysrhythmia and therefore create conflict. Still, the virtual drink seems a relatively harmless way of allowing some decompression outside the workspace, and has the positive additional implications of placing less peer pressure on those who don't want to drink, and removing the necessity of getting home – for those who drive and like a drink, that is a benefit, for those of us who like a drink and view the getting home as an increasingly painful obstacle to be avoided, again, the outcome is positive.
