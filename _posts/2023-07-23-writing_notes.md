you might ask yourself as a writer “what has changed?” at the end of a story or novel (i’m not totally convinced as satire does something v different)

the turn at the end of the first chapter of *The English Understand Wool* where it turns out the narrator is actually writing for their editor. 

the turn at the end of the novel where it is evident that one of the things that has changed is the realisation she can go to the thai tailor in Paris because her surrogate parents will have expected this. A form of trust in their ability to reconstitute (partly through islamic disguise).
